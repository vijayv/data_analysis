In brief:
1.7 mile balloon hike on edge of residential neighborhood, includes loop through quiet woods.

Distance, category, and difficulty:
This 1.7 mile balloon hike (an out and back with a loop at one end), is easy, with about 600 feet is elevation change.

Exposure:
Almost completely exposed.

Trail traffic:
Light.

Trail surfaces:
Dirt trails.

Hiking time:
1 hour.

Season:
Nice any time.

Getting there:
From Interstate 280 in San Mateo County, exit Sand Hill Road. Drive west about 3.3 miles (Sand Hill Road seamlessly turns into Portola Road along the way), then turn right onto Hayfields Road. Proceed about 0.15 mile uphill, to an unpromising-looking pullout on the right, just before Hayfields Road becomes a private lane.

Get driving or public transit directions from Transit and Trails:
http://www.transitandtrails.org/trailheads/424

GPS Coordinates* for Trailhead:
Latitude 37°23'19.21"N
Longitude 122°14'23.80"W
(* based on Google Earth data, shown as degrees, minutes, seconds)

Gas, food, and lodging:
Pay phones, gas, stores, and restaurants in Portola Valley. No camping.

Trailhead details:
Parking for 2-3 vehicles in a paved pullout. No entrance or parking fees. No facilities or maps. No designated handicapped parking, and the trail is not suitable to wheelchairs.

Rules:
These trails are open to hikers and equestrians. Pets on leash are permitted. This is an easement trail, so stay on the path.
