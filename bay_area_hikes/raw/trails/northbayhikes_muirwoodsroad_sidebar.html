In brief:
7.2 mile loop through Muir Woods and surrounding coastal scrub. Dipsea Trail is a Bay Area Ridge Trail segment.

Distance, category, and difficulty:
This 7.2 mile loop hike is moderate. Trailhead elevation is about 490 feet. The featured hike climbs to about 1300 feet, descends to 100 feet, then climbs back to the trailhead. Total elevation change is about 1300 feet. Most of the elevation change is moderate.

Exposure:
Mix of sun and shade.

Trail traffic:
Moderate-heavy.

Trail surfaces:
Dirt fire roads and trails.

Hiking time:
3 1/2 hours.

Season:
Good anytime; water levels at Redwood Creek can be high in winter.

Getting there:

From US 101 in Marin, exit CA 1/Stinson Beach, and drive about 1 mile to the junction with Almonte. Turn left to stay on Shoreline, and drive about 6.5 miles to a pullout on the left side of the road.

Gas, food, and lodging:
Gas, stores, and restaurants back off Shoreline in Mill Valley. Some services available in Stinson Beach and Muir Beach. No camping in Muir Woods; Mt. Tam State Park's has walk in sites.

GPS coordinates* for trailhead:
Latitude 37°52'10.97"N
Longitude 122°35'23.44"W
(* based on Google Earth data, shown as degrees, minutes, seconds)

Trailhead details:
Parking for about a dozen cars on the side of the road. No entrance or parking fees. No toilet facilities, drinking water, or maps available. Pay phone at Pantoll or Muir Woods. There are no designated handicapped parking spots, and trails are poorly suited to wheelchairs or strollers. There is no direct public transportation to this trailhead.

Rules:
Some trails are signed closed to bicycles, and others are multi-use. Dogs are not permitted on these trails.
